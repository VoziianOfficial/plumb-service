What Cookies Are

Cookies are small data files that may be stored on a browser or device when a user visits a website. Similar technologies may include pixels, tags, local storage, session identifiers, and other tools used for site operation, analytics, attribution, or experience improvements.

On a lead-generation platform like BlueRoute, these technologies may help understand how homeowners move from landing pages to phone calls, estimate requests, and other high-intent actions.

How BlueRoute May Use Cookies

Cookies and similar technologies may be used to keep the site working properly, understand traffic patterns, improve homeowner experience, and evaluate how well different service pages and calls to action perform.

  • To support navigation, page performance, and site stability.
  • To remember certain browser-level settings or interface preferences.
  • To analyze how visitors move through service pages, forms, and conversion-focused sections.
  • To better understand whether users interacted with emergency, drain, leak detection, water heater, fixture, or installation-related content before taking action.

Types of Cookies That May Be Used

Essential Cookies

These may help core site features function correctly, including navigation, interface stability, or security-related behavior.

Performance and Analytics Cookies

These may help measure page traffic, session patterns, and engagement so the platform can be improved over time.

Attribution and Measurement Cookies

These may help determine which channels, campaigns, landing pages, or service categories contributed to a call or estimate request.

Preference Cookies

These may be used to remember certain user settings or display preferences, depending on the implementation in use.

Lead Attribution, Call Tracking, and Routing Measurement

Because BlueRoute operates in the ready-to-buy plumbing lead-generation space, some cookies or related technologies may support attribution and conversion measurement tied to request forms and tracked phone numbers.

  • These tools may help show whether a request came from a paid ad, referral, local landing page, or another traffic source.
  • They may also help measure which page category was viewed before a call or form submission, such as emergency help, drain cleaning, or leak detection.
  • BlueRoute may use this information to improve page layouts, request flows, contractor routing logic, and marketing efficiency.

Attribution data is used to support platform operations and performance analysis. It does not change the fact that the actual plumbing work, if any, is handled by independent providers rather than BlueRoute.

Third-Party Services

BlueRoute may use third-party tools, content delivery services, fonts, analytics providers, call-tracking partners, hosting vendors, or form-processing systems that can set or access cookies according to their own practices and policies.

Those third parties may collect device-level, browser-level, or usage-level information consistent with their own documentation. Users should review the applicable third-party privacy or cookie notices where relevant.

Managing Cookie Preferences

Most browsers allow users to review, block, or delete cookies. If cookies are disabled, some parts of the site may not function as intended, and measurement of calls, forms, or landing-page performance may become less accurate.

  • Users can often manage cookies through browser settings, privacy controls, or device-specific tools.
  • Deleting or blocking cookies may reset preferences or reduce consistency across visits.
  • Users may also review the Privacy Policy for broader information-handling details.

Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers provide a “Do Not Track” setting or similar preference. Because there is not a universally accepted industry standard for responding to those signals, BlueRoute may not respond to all such settings in a uniform way.

Users who want stronger control over cookies should use the browser- or device-level settings available to them in addition to any on-site controls that may be provided.
