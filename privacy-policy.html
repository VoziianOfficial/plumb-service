Information We Collect

When a homeowner uses BlueRoute Plumbing Network, the platform may collect information provided directly by the user and information created through browsing, form activity, and call-routing behavior.

  • Directly provided information may include full name, phone number, ZIP code, requested service category, and any message or property details entered into a form.
  • Technical information may include IP address, browser type, device information, pages viewed, approximate location derived from IP, referrer information, and similar usage signals.
  • Interaction information may include the page a user visited before submitting a request, which service category was selected, whether a phone CTA or estimate form was used, and timestamps tied to that activity.

Because BlueRoute focuses on plumbing-related homeowner intent, submitted details may also describe issue urgency, fixture type, water-related damage concerns, installation requests, replacement requests, or other notes that help categorize the lead.

Request Forms, Call Data, and Routing Activity

BlueRoute operates as a connection platform, so request data and call activity are central to how the service works. When a user submits a form or calls through a tracked number on the site, the platform may log information necessary to route, attribute, and report on that interaction.

  • Estimate requests may include homeowner contact details, property ZIP code, service category, and free-form descriptions of the plumbing issue or project.
  • Call-related information may include call date and time, duration, routing destination, caller status, and limited metadata used for lead handling, quality review, fraud prevention, and performance reporting.
  • Where permitted by applicable law and where proper notice is provided, calls may be monitored or recorded for quality assurance, dispute resolution, training, or routing verification purposes.

BlueRoute may also maintain internal records showing whether a request came from an emergency plumbing page, a drain-related page, a leak-detection page, or another service category so that routing logic can better match homeowner intent.

How We Use Information

BlueRoute may use collected information to operate the platform, improve routing quality, support homeowner-to-provider connections, and maintain reliable lead-generation systems.

  • To receive, review, and route calls or estimate requests.
  • To match homeowner intent with local independent plumbing contractors or service providers.
  • To prioritize urgent plumbing request categories, including active leaks, backups, and similar time-sensitive issues.
  • To maintain platform security, reduce spam, detect misuse, and improve site performance.
  • To measure call activity, estimate form performance, page engagement, and overall platform usage.
  • To communicate about a submitted request, respond to support inquiries, and maintain internal business records.

Information may also be used in aggregated or de-identified form to understand which service categories, request paths, and local routing zones perform best over time.

How Request Information May Be Shared

BlueRoute is a connection platform, not the contractor performing plumbing work. To support follow-up, relevant request details may be shared with independent providers and certain operational vendors participating in active routing areas.

  • Shared information may include name, phone number, ZIP code, requested service category, urgency, and issue or project notes.
  • Relevant data may be shared with call-routing providers, form-processing vendors, analytics providers, cloud hosting providers, or other service partners that support site operations.
  • Independent providers are responsible for their own estimate process, scheduling, communications, and work performed.
  • BlueRoute does not guarantee that every request will receive a response or that every provider will be available in every market.

BlueRoute may also disclose information if required to do so by law, in response to legal process, to protect platform rights or security, or in connection with a business transaction involving all or part of the platform.

BlueRoute does not represent itself as the plumbing company doing the work. The role of the platform is to organize and route homeowner demand.

Cookies and Analytics

The site may use cookies and similar technologies to keep the platform functional, remember certain preferences, measure visitor engagement, and understand how homeowners move through service pages and request flows.

  • Cookies may help measure whether a visitor reached the site through advertising, organic search, referral traffic, or another source.
  • Analytics-related tools may help BlueRoute understand which plumbing categories, calls to action, and landing-page layouts generate meaningful homeowner engagement.
  • Preference-related tools may help preserve interface settings, device-level behavior, or other convenience features depending on the implementation in use.

Additional details about cookie usage are available in the Cookie Policy.

Retention and Security

BlueRoute may retain information for as long as reasonably needed to operate the platform, maintain records, investigate disputes, comply with applicable obligations, support contractor-routing reporting, and improve lead-handling systems.

Different types of data may be retained for different periods depending on their role in fraud prevention, call attribution, customer support, reporting, accounting, or legal compliance. When information is no longer reasonably needed, BlueRoute may delete, anonymize, or aggregate it.

Reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards may be used, but no website, database, or transmission method can be guaranteed to be completely secure.

State Privacy Rights and Additional Disclosures

Depending on where a homeowner lives, applicable state privacy laws may provide rights related to access, correction, deletion, or limitation of certain data uses. BlueRoute will review privacy-related requests in light of applicable law and operational obligations.

  • Users may request information about the categories of personal information collected and the categories of third parties with whom information may be shared.
  • Users may request correction of inaccurate information that they previously submitted through the platform.
  • Users may request deletion of certain data, subject to exceptions related to legal compliance, security, fraud prevention, operational necessity, or recordkeeping.

BlueRoute does not sell personal information in the ordinary sense of selling a homeowner list to the public, but information may be shared as part of the lead-routing and provider-connection model described in this policy.

Children's Privacy

BlueRoute is intended for homeowners and other adult users seeking local plumbing-related connections. The site is not directed to children under 18, and BlueRoute does not knowingly collect personal information from children through its standard request paths.

If BlueRoute becomes aware that personal information from a child has been submitted through the site without proper authorization, reasonable steps may be taken to delete that information.

Your Choices, Requests, and Contact Information

Users may choose not to submit information through forms, may use browser settings to manage certain cookies, and may contact the platform with privacy questions or requests related to personal information.

Privacy-related requests should include enough detail for BlueRoute to understand and evaluate the request, such as the contact information previously submitted and the nature of the inquiry.
